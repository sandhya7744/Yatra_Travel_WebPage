2. Red Fort

* Red Fort is a historic fort in the city of Delhi in India. It was the main residence of the emperors of the Mughal dynasty for nearly 200 years, until 1856. It is located in the center of Delhi and houses a number of museums. It was Constructed in 1639 by the fifth Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan as the palace of his fortified capital Shahjahanabad, the Red Fort is named for its massive enclosing walls of red sandstone and is adjacent to the older Salimgarh Fort, built by Islam Shah Suri in 1546 AD.

Red fort is historical place located in the center of New Delhi banks of Yamuna river. 4. Lotus Temple

* The Lotus Temple in Delhi, India, is a jaw-dropping work of architecture that effortlessly combines spirituality, beauty, and human unity. From its dazzling lotus-molded design to its peaceful atmosphere, the Lotus Sanctuary is a desert spring that merits the most note worthy applause.

* In many cultures, the lotus flower is a symbol of purity and enlightenment. The nine petals of the temple gracefully unfold to form a central prayer hall. During the day, the temple's flawless white marble exterior reflects sunlight, giving it a radiant glow that highlights its ethereal beauty. The temple became a captivating sight as the evening wore on, illuminated by soft, warm lights that highlighted its architectural finesse and promoted tranquility.

* The Lotus Temple's emphasis on environmental sustainability is one of its most striking features. The innovative architecture of the temple raises its ecological consciousness by incorporating eco-friendly features like rainwater harvesting and natural ventilation systems. The temple's gardens and landscaping add to its natural beauty, blending architecture and nature in a harmonious way.

* The Lotus Temple is more than just a tourist spot; It demonstrates the value of human connection, spirituality, and unity. The temple's universal message of harmony and peace is profoundly moving and relevant regardless of one's spiritual or religious background. It encourages visitors to embrace diversity and foster understanding and serves as a gentle reminder of the thread that unites humanity.
